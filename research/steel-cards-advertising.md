# Advertising & audience playbook — TWAIN metal pair-cards

*Support note for [09 #11](../09-idea-board.md) and the
[site](../couples-cards/index.html). The product is emotional and visual, sold in
**pairs** — which means the ad is the moment, not the spec. This note maps the buyable
**audiences**, the **angles** that pull each one, the **creative** that sells a shiny
object, and the **channels** to run it on.*

The core insight: **"made for two" is a positioning, not a limit.** Any two people who
want to carry each other are the market — couples are the front door, but *parents*,
*grief*, and *distance* are bigger rooms behind it.

---

## 1. Audience segments (ranked by pull)

Each is a distinct ad set with its own creative, occasion and copy. Never run one
generic "buy a metal card" ad — run the *moment*.

### 🥇 A — New parents *(the standout — highest emotion, clearest trigger)*
A newborn is the single strongest gifting trigger there is: intense emotion, a fixed
date, two new parents who each want the baby with them, and grandparents desperate to
spend. The **pair** mechanic fits perfectly — **one card for mum, one for dad**, the
baby's first photo carried by both.

- **Angle:** *"They're with you, even at your desk."* The parent back at work, opening
  their wallet, the baby's face in steel. Colourise the hospital photo; add name, date,
  time, weight, birth coordinates along the edge.
- **Extensions:** a **three-card** family option (mum · dad · grandparent); "first
  photo" keepsake; a Father's/Mother's-Day push using the same asset.
- **Where they are:** Instagram/Facebook (parenting interest + life-event "new parent"
  targeting), TikTok #newbornphotography / #firsttimeparents, Pinterest "newborn keepsake
  / new dad gift", mum-and-baby newsletters and influencers, NCT-style communities.
- **Why it wins:** highest willingness-to-pay, built-in date, and it *doubles the order*
  (two parents = the pair sells itself).

### 🥈 B — Couples & anniversaries *(the home base)*
The positioning the [site](../couples-cards/index.html) is built on. Two people, one
moment, carried apart.

- **Angle:** *"Carry each other, everywhere."* First date, wedding, the day you met —
  coordinates + date on brushed steel.
- **Occasions to buy against:** anniversaries, Valentine's, engagements, weddings,
  first-apartment. Each is its own dated campaign.
- **Where:** IG/TikTok couples content, wedding/engagement targeting, Pinterest
  "anniversary gift for him/her", Etsy for intent-led search demand.

### 🥉 C — Long-distance *(couples, students, military, migrant families)*
Distance is the purest expression of "the same thing, apart."

- **Angle:** *"Different city. Same moment, in your pocket."* Split-shipping to two
  addresses is a native feature to lead with.
- **Where:** #longdistance communities, student targeting, military-family groups,
  diaspora communities.

### D — Memorial & remembrance *(handle with real care)*
Carrying someone who's gone — a genuinely powerful, high-intent use. Steel = permanence
reads as "they're still with me."

- **Angle:** quiet, respectful — *"Keep them close."* Restore/colourise an old photo.
- **Guardrail:** sombre creative, never salesy; separate landing copy. This is a moment,
  not a promo. Keep it honest and gentle ([07](../07-guardrails.md)).

### E — Pets
Same emotional mechanics as couples/parents, lighter tone, huge social reach.
- **Angle:** *"Best friend, always in your pocket."* Two cards → one for each owner, or
  keepsake after a pet passes.

### F — Self-gift / travel & places
Not two-people, but two-*things*: a place and you. Lower priority; a long-tail expansion.

## 2. The angle formula

Every ad = **[a specific two] + [a specific moment] + [the permanence of steel]**.
That's the whole template. Fill the brackets per segment:

| Segment | The two | The moment | The line |
|---|---|---|---|
| New parents | mum + dad | the birth | *"They're with you, even at your desk."* |
| Couples | you + them | the day you met | *"Carry each other, everywhere."* |
| Long-distance | here + there | every day apart | *"Same moment, two cities."* |
| Memorial | you + them | a life | *"Keep them close."* |
| Pets | you + them | best friend | *"Always in your pocket."* |

## 3. Creative direction (selling a shiny object)

A metal card is a **video product** — it catches light, it has weight. Lead with motion,
not stills.

- **Hero shot:** the card sliding *out of a real wallet*, catching a light sweep — mirrors
  the site's sheen animation. This one shot is the whole brand.
- **The reveal:** hands opening the linen box to **two** cards. The "two" is the surprise.
- **Before/after:** a faded/old photo → the colourised steel card. Huge for parents &
  memorial. (Only ever show *real* before/afters — no faked transformations,
  [07](../07-guardrails.md).)
- **UGC / creator:** send pairs to couples & new-parent creators; their unboxing *is* the
  ad. Cheapest high-trust reach and the base of the [affiliate](../04-growth-engine.md) engine.
- **Format:** vertical 9:16, sound-led, first 1.5s = the card + light. Text overlay carries
  the moment for silent autoplay.

## 4. Channels & how to spend

| Channel | Role | Segment fit | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Instagram / Facebook Ads** | primary paid | Parents, couples, memorial | Life-event + interest targeting; retarget site + customiser drop-offs |
| **TikTok** | discovery / virality | Parents, couples, pets | The wallet-sheen video native here; creator-led |
| **Pinterest** | high-intent search | Gifts, weddings, newborn | People plan gifts here; long shelf-life pins |
| **Etsy** | intent demand + proof | Couples, memorial, pets | Capture existing "metal photo card" search while the brand builds |
| **Creator / affiliate** | trust + CAC control | All | Gifted pairs → codes; ties to [04](../04-growth-engine.md) — distribution is the game |
| **Google Search** | bottom-funnel | Gifts | Bid on "metal photo keepsake / new dad gift / anniversary steel" |
| **Email/SMS** | repeat & occasion | All | The reorder engine — see §5 |

## 5. The occasion loop (why this compounds)

A pair-card looks one-off, but the *relationships* recur. Capture the date at checkout,
then re-market the **next occasion** to an owner who already loves the product:

> bought for the birth → *first birthday* → *Father's/Mother's Day* → *anniversary* →
> *"add a grandparent to the set"*

That calendar-driven email/SMS flow is what turns a one-off keepsake into a repeat
business — the same logic as the refill angle in [09 #10](../09-idea-board.md), applied to
*occasions* instead of consumables.

## 6. Test plan (first £ spent)

1. **One product, three audiences.** Same wallet-sheen video, three copy variants:
   **new-parents**, **couples-anniversary**, **long-distance**. Small budget each.
2. Send traffic to the [customiser](../10-build-and-tooling-board.md) (or a waitlist if
   pre-launch). Measure cost-per-add-to-cart and **which moment converts cheapest**.
3. Double down on the winner; hand that winning angle to creators for UGC.
4. **New parents is the hypothesis to beat** — highest emotion, dated trigger, and it
   naturally sells the pair.

## 7. Guardrails ([07](../07-guardrails.md))

- Describe the finish and durability exactly — no "handmade in our studio" if it's
  print-to-order; no faked before/afters.
- Memorial creative stays sombre and honest, never performance-optimised into insensitivity.
- Uploaded photos of children/loved ones = sensitive personal data — clear consent/rights
  terms and GDPR handling (see the [outsourcing note §6](./steel-wallet-cards-outsourcing.md)).
