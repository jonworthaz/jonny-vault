# Product range — one photo-on-steel, many carriers

*Support note for [09 #11](../09-idea-board.md). The core asset — a **colourised photo
printed into steel** — doesn't have to live only in a wallet. The same print becomes a
family of products by changing the **carrier**. This is how a one-off keepsake grows an
AOV ladder and, crucially, a **recurring** line.*

The strategic point: the wallet card is beautiful but **one-and-done** (the law-#1 problem
flagged in [09 #11](../09-idea-board.md)). Adding a **magnet wall** fixes it — the wall is
bought once, then the *magnets that fill it* are bought again and again. That's the recurring
engine this idea was missing.

---

## The range

### 1. Wallet pair-card — *the hero* (already built)
Colour photo in brushed steel, ID-1, sold in twos. The [site](../couples-cards/index.html)
is built around this. Carried, private, everyday. **One-off.**

### 2. Fridge magnet — *the everyday, visible one* (add-on / impulse)
The exact same card with a **magnetic backing** — lives on the fridge instead of hidden in a
wallet. Seen a hundred times a day.
- **Why it sells:** lower price, impulse add-on at checkout, and *perfect for new parents* —
  the baby's face on the fridge is the most-looked-at spot in the house.
- **Bundle:** "one for the wallet, one for the fridge" — the pair mechanic, re-used.
- **Margin:** cheapest variant to add (same print + a magnetic sheet); ideal **AOV booster**.

### 3. The magnet wall — *the recurring engine* (hero of the range, strategically)
A mounted **ferromagnetic steel panel** (brushed, framed) that becomes a living gallery: you
buy the wall once, then keep buying **steel photo magnets** to add to it over time.
- **Why it matters:** the wall is the *platform*; the magnets are the *refills*. Birthdays,
  holidays, new photos → a reason to buy again forever. This is the genuinely **recurring**
  line — the same logic as the refill angle in [09 #10](../09-idea-board.md), but the
  consumable is *memories*, which never run out.
- **Formats:** small (6-magnet) · family (12) · statement wall (24+). Sell as a starter
  kit (panel + first set) then a **magnet subscription / occasion pack** on top.
- **Higher AOV + recurring + gifting** in one product. If the numbers work, this is what
  moves #11 from 🟡 watch toward a real lead.

### 4. Optional further carriers *(park unless the core proves out)*
Keychain/dog-tag, bookmark, desk stand, pet-tag. Same print, new carrier. Don't spread thin
until the wallet + magnet + wall trio is validated.

## The one technical catch (decides whether "magnet" works at all)

**Most "stainless steel" is *not* magnetic.** Austenitic stainless (304/316 — the common,
shiny grades) is **non-magnetic**, so a magnet will *not* stick to it. This affects the range
two ways:

1. **The magnets themselves** need a **magnetic backing layer** bonded to the card — that's
   fine on any substrate (works regardless of the card's own metal).
2. **The wall panel must be ferromagnetic** — i.e. **ferritic stainless (430)** or a
   coated/branded **mild-steel** panel. If you build the wall from 304 stainless, nothing
   sticks. Spec the panel as **magnet-receptive steel** and prototype it before selling.

So: cards/magnets = print + magnetic backing (easy); **wall = magnet-receptive steel, verified
with real magnets first.** Same honesty rule as the colour-on-steel question in the
[outsourcing note](./steel-wallet-cards-outsourcing.md).

## Fulfilment impact

- **Magnets:** same print process as the cards + a die-cut magnetic sheet — nearly every
  metal-print POD ([Prodigi](https://www.prodigi.com/) etc.) and photo lab already offers
  photo magnets. Lowest-effort SKU to add.
- **Wall panel:** larger flat steel + framing/mount + packaging (heavier, higher ship cost) —
  source from a metal fabricator or a magnetic-board supplier; the photo magnets ship via the
  existing card pipeline. Validate shipping cost on the panel early — big flat metal is awkward.
