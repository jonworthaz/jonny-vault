# 07 — Guardrails

*The lines we don't cross. This is not legal boilerplate — it's the risk model
that keeps the business alive. Every Medvi headline failure maps to a line here.*

## Why this is a growth document, not a compliance chore

Medvi's deceptive tactics produced short-term numbers and long-term catastrophe:
an FDA warning letter, FTC scrutiny, class actions, ad-account risk, and a brand
that's now a cautionary tale. **Those tactics were never the source of the
value** — the affiliate engine and AI cost structure were. We keep the engine and
delete the fraud. Honest businesses also keep their payment processors and ad
accounts, which is itself a decisive growth advantage.

## The do-not-cross list (mapped to Medvi's mistakes)

| # | Line | Medvi failure it prevents |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **No fake personas.** No invented "experts", no fake authors, no bot accounts posing as people. | 800+ fake doctor accounts |
| 2 | **No fabricated testimonials or deepfaked results.** Reviews come from real, consenting users. AI-generated imagery is either clearly illustrative or labelled. | Deepfake before/afters |
| 3 | **No false equivalence / unsubstantiated claims.** Every claim is true and, where it matters, substantiable. | "Same as Wegovy/Ozempic" misbranding |
| 4 | **Transparent pricing.** Trial terms, step-up price, and renewal date shown *before* payment. 30-day months. One-click cancel. | 28-day cadence + intro-step complaints |
| 5 | **Affiliate compliance.** Affiliates must disclose paid relationships (#ad / FTC + ASA rules) and may only use approved, truthful creative. We police it. | Affiliates running deceptive ads |
| 6 | **Data protection by design.** Minimal data, encryption, Stripe for cards, GDPR/CCPA basics, breach plan. | OpenLoop 1.6M-record breach |
| 7 | **Honest AI disclosure.** If support or content is AI, we don't pretend it's a named human. | AI "hallucinated" prices/products |

## Advertising standards (because we're geo-agnostic)

Geo-agnostic means multiple regimes apply at once. Bake the strictest common
denominator in:
- **US FTC**: truthful, substantiated, clear disclosures, no fake endorsements
  (the 2023 endorsement guides + the rule on fake reviews).
- **UK ASA/CAP** + **CMA** (fake reviews now unlawful under the DMCC Act).
- **EU**: UCPD / Digital Services Act basics.

Practical rule: **if a claim would embarrass us in a screenshot, we don't run it.**

## Subscription / "dark pattern" hygiene

Regulators (FTC "click-to-cancel", UK CMA, EU) are actively targeting
subscription traps. We pre-comply:
- Clear consent for recurring billing.
- Reminder before each renewal (and before trial→paid).
- Cancel in as few clicks as sign-up.
- No hidden fees, no padded billing cycles.

This *reduces* churn complaints and chargebacks — good ethics and good economics.

## The compliance bench (cheap insurance)

- A part-time **advertising/consumer-law advisor** reviews the funnel + ad claims
  before scale (a few hours, a few hundred $).
- Standard **terms, privacy policy, refund policy** drafted properly once.
- A simple **breach + complaint response plan** written before we need it.

## The test we apply to every growth idea

> "Does this win because it's genuinely better/cheaper/faster — or because the
> customer is being misled?" If it's the second, it's a Medvi-style time bomb.
> We don't ship it.
