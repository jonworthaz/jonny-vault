## The three-layer model

Medvi is not a medical company. It's a **marketing + billing company** sitting on
top of someone else's regulated infrastructure.

```
LAYER 1 — ACQUISITION  (Medvi owns this. This is the moat.)
  Affiliates (10k+) · Paid social · AI-generated creative · Quiz funnel
        │
LAYER 2 — DELIVERY  (Fully outsourced. Commodity.)
  CareValidate (white-label telehealth platform)
  OpenLoop Health (licensed physician network)
  Belmar Pharma (compounding + fulfilment + shipping)
        │
LAYER 3 — BILLING & RETENTION  (Medvi owns this. This is the cash.)
  Subscription billing · Auto-renew (28-day) · AI customer service
```

**The insight:** Layers 1 and 3 are where margin and customer ownership live.
Layer 2 is capital-intensive, regulated, and commoditised — so they rent it.

## How each piece worked

### Distribution = affiliates (the real engine)
- 10,000+ affiliates.
- Paid **$300–$500 per approved customer** vs. ~$75 industry norm.
- Could outbid everyone because the margin + recurring revenue covered it.
- This is the single most replicable, most powerful element.

### Creative = AI at zero marginal cost
- ChatGPT / Claude / Grok for code, copy, support scripts.
- MidJourney / Runway for ad images and video.
- 5,000+ active Meta ads at peak.
- Near-zero creative production cost → could flood every channel.

### Funnel
```
Affiliate/Ad → Quiz (eligibility + commitment) → Payment captured
→ Provider review → Fulfilment → 28-day auto-renew → AI support/retention
```

### Pricing hook
- $179 first month → $299/month after (≈40% step-up).
- 28-day cycle = ~13 charges/year, not 12 (~8% more than implied).
- The #1 source of customer complaints.

### Economics (sector benchmark)
- GLP-1 CAC ~$180–$320; net ~$300/mo/patient → ~1-month payback.
- **Everything hinges on 6-month retention clearing ~50%.** The risk was never
  CAC — it was churn (and regulatory exposure to the compounding loophole).

## What's killing Medvi (the do-NOT-copy list)

| Problem | What happened |
|---|---|
| FDA warning letter (Feb 2026) | Misbranding compounded drugs; "Same active ingredient as Wegovy/Ozempic" implied FDA approval |
| Fake physician personas | 800+ alleged fake "doctor" Facebook accounts; ads under invented names |
| Deepfake testimonials | AI-generated before/after photos, fabricated video reviews |
| Class actions | Partners sued over "snake oil" oral GLP-1 |
| Data breach (via OpenLoop) | 1.6M records claimed accessed; lawsuits |
| Pricing complaints | Hidden 28-day cadence, intro→full step-up |

These created the headline numbers *and* the existential risk. They are
separable from the operating model. **We take the model, not the shortcuts.**

## What we keep (the copy list)

1. **2-person + AI operating model** — labour cost near zero.
2. **Outsource the capital-intensive delivery layer** — stay asset-light.
3. **Own acquisition + billing** — the two profit centres.
4. **Affiliate-first distribution at above-market commissions.**
5. **AI-generated creative and support** at zero marginal cost.
6. **Subscription with an intro hook** — but transparent (see Guardrails).
7. **A quiz/assessment funnel** that qualifies and commits the buyer.
8. **Ride an existing demand wave** rather than create demand.
