# 04 — The Growth Engine

*Distribution is the moat. This is the part of Medvi that actually mattered.*

## Strategy: outsource distribution, don't build it

Medvi didn't build a big marketing team — it built a **market of marketers**
(10k+ affiliates) and paid them more than anyone else could. We do the same, with
a margin (digital, ~95%) that lets us out-pay competitors *structurally*.

Three channels, in priority order:

```
1. AFFILIATES      ← the engine (owned relationships, performance-paid)
2. PAID SOCIAL     ← the throttle (turn up when payback is proven)
3. AI CONTENT/SEO  ← the flywheel (compounding, near-free, slow)
```

## 1. Affiliate program (build first)

### Mechanics
- **Commission: recurring 30–40% for the life of the customer**, OR a high
  one-time CPA (e.g. $40–$80) — test both. Recurring aligns affiliates with
  retention; CPA pays out faster and attracts media buyers.
- Because margin is ~95%, a 40% recurring payout still leaves us ~55% — Medvi
  netted far less per dollar and still out-bid the market.
- **Tiered bumps**: top performers get higher rates / bonuses. Manufactured
  status + income = loyalty.

### Infrastructure
- Affiliate platform: **Rewardful, FirstPromoter, or Tolt** (Stripe-native,
  cheap, handles tracking + payouts).
- Optional reach into CPA networks once the funnel converts (where Medvi found
  scale): list the offer so professional media buyers can run it.

### Recruitment (who, and how we find them)
| Affiliate type | Where | Pitch |
|---|---|---|
| Niche creators/influencers | YouTube, TikTok, X, newsletters in the niche | "Highest recurring payout in the category + a tool your audience needs" |
| Micro-communities | Discords, subreddits, FB groups | Affiliate + exclusive deal for members |
| Media buyers | CPA networks, affiliate forums | High CPA + clean, converting funnel |
| Existing customers | In-product | Refer-a-friend = give a month, get a month |

### The non-negotiable
Every affiliate must use **clear #ad / "I earn a commission" disclosure** and may
only use **approved, truthful creative** (assets we supply). This is exactly where
Medvi blew up. Compliance here is a growth *enabler* — it's what keeps the ad
accounts and the brand alive. See [07 — Guardrails](./07-guardrails.md).

## 2. Paid social (the throttle)

- Start only after the funnel converts organically/via affiliates.
- Channels: Meta + TikTok + (for B2B niches) YouTube/X.
- **AI creative pipeline** lets us run dozens of angles cheaply — *but every claim
  is real and every face is consented/licensed or clearly synthetic-labelled.*
- Rule: scale spend strictly to proven payback (see Economics). No "growth at any
  cost" — that's how CAC quietly buries you.

## 3. AI content + SEO (the flywheel)

- Programmatic + editorial content targeting the buyer's problem keywords.
- The product's own AI generates a large surface of genuinely useful free
  content (templates, calculators, mini-tools) → top-of-funnel + backlinks.
- Compounds for free over months; insurance against ad-platform dependency
  (a risk that has burned many D2C brands, Medvi included).

## The creative machine (how 2 people flood every channel)

```
Idea/angle (AI brainstorm)
   → Copy variants (LLM)
   → Image/video variants (image+video models)
   → Landing page variant (AI-coded)
   → Ship to affiliate library + ad manager
   → Measure → kill losers → scale winners
```
This is Medvi's "5,000 ads" capability — minus the fake doctors. Volume of
*honest* creative is a totally legitimate, durable advantage.

## Channel sequencing

| Phase | Primary | Secondary |
|---|---|---|
| Validate | 5–20 hand-picked affiliates | manual outreach |
| Early scale | Affiliate program open | small paid tests |
| Scale | Paid social throttled to payback | CPA networks |
| Durable | All three | SEO flywheel matured |
